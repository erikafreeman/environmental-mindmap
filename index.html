Article 6.2 - Bilateral Trading

Country-to-Country Trading: Article 6.2 allows countries to trade emission reductions through bilateral agreements. These traded credits are called Internationally Transferred Mitigation Outcomes (ITMOs).

Requirements:

  • Host country authorization
  • Corresponding adjustments in national accounts
  • Robust accounting to prevent double counting
  • Sustainable development co-benefits
Article 6.4 - Centralized Mechanism

UN-Supervised Trading: The Paris Agreement Crediting Mechanism (PACM) replaces the CDM under UN oversight. Credits are called Article 6.4 Emission Reductions ('6.4 ERs').

COP29 Breakthrough

Implementation Ready: COP29 resolved key issues including revocation clauses and CDM credit transfers. Once ITMOs have been "first transferred," they cannot be affected by revocations unless specified by Parties.

Market Demand

Massive Scale Needed: In 2024, 12 international registries issued 305 million carbon credits total - just a fraction of what will be needed under CORSIA. Demand could exceed 100 Mt CO2-eq annually before 2030.

Approved Standards (December 2024)

Expanded Eligibility: Four additional standards received ICAO approval: Gold Standard, Verra, Climate Action Reserve, and Global Carbon Council join previously approved ACR and ART.

Eligibility Requirements:

  • 2021-2026 vintages for Phase 1
  • Host country Letter of Authorization
  • Corresponding adjustments applied
  • Standards must meet CORSIA EUC

Growth Drivers

Multiple Catalysts: Growth drivers include mandatory reporting under CORSIA, potential convergence of compliance and voluntary markets via Article 6, and increasing demand for carbon removal within financial sectors.

Market Drivers:

  • Corporate Net Zero: Private sector climate commitments

Corporate Participation
